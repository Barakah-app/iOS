Privacy Policy

Short version: your data never leaves your device and iCloud account.

Last updated: June 26, 2026

✅ The short version

  • Barakah has no account, no login, and no sign-up.
  • All data is stored on your iPhone/iPad and in your own private iCloud account.
  • We do not operate any server that stores your data.
  • We do not sell, share, or monetize your data in any way.
  • We do not include any third-party analytics or advertising SDKs.
  • You can delete all data at any time by deleting the app.

1. Who we are

Barakah ("the app") is developed and published by AbdurRahman Rozell / Barakah Corp. For questions about this policy, contact us at barakah-app@proton.me.

2. What data the app collects

Barakah stores data you enter about your children and family. This may include:

  • Child information: name, date of birth, gender, photo (optional)
  • Health & care logs: feeding records (type, amount, time), sleep records (duration, type), diaper changes, growth measurements (weight, length), medications and dosages, milestones
  • Family member information: names and role (parent, caregiver, viewer) for people you invite to share the app
  • App preferences: display units, language, reminder settings

This data is entered by you and remains entirely under your control.

3. Where data is stored

All data is stored locally on your device using Apple's Core Data framework and, if you choose to enable iCloud, in your personal iCloud private database via Apple CloudKit. We have no access to your iCloud data — Apple's privacy infrastructure ensures only you and devices signed into your iCloud account can read it.

We do not operate any application server, cloud database, or backend API.

4. iCloud sync and Family Sharing

When iCloud sync is enabled, Barakah uses Apple CloudKit to synchronize your data across your personal devices. If you invite family members or caregivers using the in-app "Invite Member" feature, a CKShare link is generated. Anyone with that link and an iCloud account can join your family group and see or edit data according to the role you assign them (parent, caregiver, viewer, etc.).

The sync happens entirely through Apple's infrastructure. Barakah Corp does not receive, process, or store any synced data.

5. Device identifiers

Barakah stores a device identifier in the system Keychain to associate a device with a Family Member record. This identifier is derived from Apple's UIDevice.identifierForVendor and persists across reinstalls. It is used only to distinguish devices within your own family group and is never transmitted to any server operated by Barakah Corp.

6. Local notifications

If you enable reminders, Barakah schedules local notifications through Apple's UserNotifications framework. No notification data is transmitted off your device.

7. Home Screen Widget

The "Last Fed" widget reads feeding data from a shared App Group container (a private storage area on your device accessible only to the Barakah app and its widget extension). No data is sent outside the device.

8. Siri and App Intents

When you use Siri shortcuts ("Log a feeding in Barakah"), the Siri request is processed by Apple on-device or through Apple's Siri infrastructure according to Apple's own privacy policy. Barakah's App Intents code runs locally inside the app process and writes directly to the local data store. We do not receive or process voice data.

9. No third-party tracking or advertising

Barakah does not include any advertising SDK, analytics SDK, or third-party tracking library. There are no ads of any kind.

10. In-app purchases

Barakah Premium subscriptions are processed entirely by Apple's App Store. Barakah Corp does not collect or store payment information. Apple may process purchase-related data per their own privacy policy.

11. Children's privacy

Barakah is a tool designed for parents and caregivers. It does not collect data from children directly — all data is entered by the adult user. The app does not connect children to any online service and does not transmit any data relating to children to Barakah Corp or any third party.

12. Data deletion

To delete all your data:

  • From this device: Settings → Log Out, or delete the app from your iPhone/iPad.
  • From iCloud: Go to iPhone Settings → [your name] → iCloud → Manage Account Storage → Barakah → Delete Data, or delete the iCloud account data at iCloud.com.

Deleting the app removes local data immediately. Removing iCloud data removes it from all synced devices.

13. Security

Barakah supports Face ID, Touch ID, and device passcode authentication to prevent unauthorized access. The app lock is enforced every time the app is opened from background. The device identifier is stored in the Keychain with kSecAttrAccessibleAfterFirstUnlockThisDeviceOnly protection, so it stays on this device only and is never included in encrypted backups or transferred to another device.

14. Changes to this policy

If we make material changes to this policy, we will update the date at the top of this page. The current policy is always available at this URL. Continued use of the app after changes constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.

15. Contact

Questions or concerns about privacy? Email us at barakah-app@proton.me.
